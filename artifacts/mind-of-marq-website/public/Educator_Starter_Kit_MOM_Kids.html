Section 1 · Story Inspiration

Percy's First Resolution

A young penguin sets a goal and discovers that growth comes through practice, failure, and persistence. Students explore how determination and curiosity lead to new discoveries. Percy trains, experiments, and keeps trying even after setbacks.

Goal Setting Resilience STEM Curiosity Ages 6–8
Section 2 · STEM Exploration Activity

Gravity and Motion

Ask students: Why can birds fly but penguins cannot? Discuss gravity and wing structure.

Hands-On Activity
Paper Wing Experiment

Have students experiment with paper wings. Try three wing shapes:

1
Flat — drop and observe movement
2
Curved — drop and observe movement
3
Wide — drop and observe movement
Discussion Questions
  • Which wing shape stayed in the air longest?
  • Why do you think that happened?
Learning Focus
Observation Experimentation Scientific Thinking
Section 3 · Social Emotional Learning

Goal Setting

Ask students: Have you ever tried something that felt impossible at first?

Discussion Prompts
  • What helped Percy keep trying?
  • Why is practice important?
  • What does it feel like when someone believes in you?
Reflection Activity
Students write one personal goal they want to try this year.
