PDF CONTENT

Title:
Story Driven STEM and SEL Classroom Starter Kit

Subtitle:
Sample activities inspired by Mind of Marq stories designed to encourage curiosity, perseverance, and self discovery.

Section 1
Story Inspiration

Heading:
Percy’s First Resolution

Text:
A young penguin sets a goal and discovers that growth comes through practice, failure, and persistence.

Students explore how determination and curiosity lead to new discoveries. Percy trains, experiments, and keeps trying even after setbacks.

Section 2
STEM Exploration Activity

Heading:
Gravity and Motion

Text:
Ask students:
Why can birds fly but penguins cannot?

Discuss gravity and wing structure.

Activity:
Have students experiment with paper wings.

Try three wing shapes:
Flat
Curved
Wide

Drop each design and observe how they move.

Discussion questions:
Which wing shape stayed in the air the longest
Why do you think that happened

Learning focus:
Observation
Experimentation
Scientific thinking

Section 3
Social Emotional Learning Discussion

Heading:
Goal Setting

Text:
Ask students:
Have you ever tried something that felt impossible at first?

Discussion prompts:
What helped Percy keep trying
Why is practice important
What does it feel like when someone believes in you

Reflection:
Students write one personal goal they want to try this year.

Section 4
Nature and Growth Activity

Heading:
Caterpillar Transformation

Text:
Inspired by The Brief but Spectacular Lives of Caterpillars.

Ask students:
How do animals change as they grow?

Activity:
Students draw four stages of a butterfly life cycle.

Egg
Caterpillar
Chrysalis
Butterfly

Discussion:
What changes during transformation
How do people grow and change too

Section 5
Movement and Balance

Heading:
Inspired by Confessions of a Teenaged Martial Artist

Text:
Discuss how balance, posture, and body control help athletes.

Activity:
Have students stand on one foot for ten seconds.

Then try again with arms extended.

Discuss:
Why does balance matter in sports and movement?

Closing line:
Stories help students explore science, understand emotions, and imagine new possibilities.

Mind of Marq Kids combines storytelling with learning experiences that support both STEM curiosity and social emotional growth.
